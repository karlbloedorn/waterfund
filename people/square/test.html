Diego Marquez
Farmer
Diego owns a 3-hectacre area of land on which he has a farm and a house. He has had to give up a significant portion of his land in order to protect his only water source, a set of springs.
Ediberto Perea
Farmer
Ediberto helped establish a silvopasture system, that combines grazing and forestry, in his family farm. He has seen multiple benefits: decrease of erosion and degradation of water quality and increase in shade for his livestock.
Naomi Catalina Beltran
Ranch Owner
Naomi owns a ranch with springs, native forests, and a fault scarp - a raised ridge caused by an earthquake zone that is very prone to erosion. Keeping eroded soil out of streams is a challenge that Naomi and Asobolo have been working together to overcome.
Manuel Esteban Guefias Salazar

Esteban considers it is his identity and “duty” to work to conserve nature and biodiversity on his farm. He is acutely aware of these species nearly going extinct due to large-scale deforestation generations ago.
